Crystal Collection

Client: This site is for the business, Crystal Collection and they sell healing crystals/stones to people who are interested in its healing properties and to those who find the crystals and stones just “aesthetically pleasing” or just for pure decoration.

Problem: This business needs a place to sell their products and a space where customers can request items, and contact them easily.

Solution (Goal): The solution is to create a website that can display their products, location and other customer needs.

Roles: UI designer, Prototyper and Web Designer

Tools: Adobe XD, Dreamweaver, Wordpress

Skills: Wireframing, Prototyping, Usability Testing

Time: One Week



Design Process:

Plan ---> Information Architecture ---> Visual Identity ---> Wireframing ---> Prototyping ---> Finalize



Plan (Thinking Steps)


Visual Identity




Information Architecture



The brand’s visual identity is a purple/lilac colour. This is because one of the meanings of this colour, intended to stick with this company and it visual identity with is “happy” and “passion”. Not only does this colour represent this meaning but this colour is very similar to the crystal amethyst. The brand wants the crystal amethyst to remind the customers of the brand as helpful, as the (healing property) amethyst helps with almost everything such as dispelling anger, rage, fear and anxiety. The brand wants customers to feel comfortable when searching the website and to remind them that the company is here for them and is ready to help them with their needs.







Homepage: The homepage has working navigation across all the inner pages and features a mash-up of highlight content from all the inner pages as well as the blog. The featured highlights are presented visually appealing and in an engaging way and uses at least 3 rich media components as the requirements of the project state: carousel, slider, connotative background video, and custom map.

1 landing blog page aggregating and linking to a few individual blog posts:

Shown on the Homepage and under the About section: The blog is essentially a page where the business posts reviews they have received (real-time) from their customers via order notes, email or comments left under their videos or images from social media.

1 event registration section – About: The About page has details about the company, and where they are based from. It also includes information such as pickup, and shipping options. The page also mentions the refund policy for Crystal Collection. It also lets users to register and become part of the “Crystal Collection family” and they are able to let the company know what crystals the user would like to see in the store. The about page also has dropdown menu which leads to the blog.







Packaging Orders: This page displays the company’s TikTok-social media account. The page embeds videos from the company’s social media account which exhibits how they package their crystals. It shows a “unique” side of the company and customers like seeing interactive content.(Static page does not work correctly)

1 contact information page with integrated form – Contact: The contact page has the option for users who visit the website to input their personal information to send an email to the company. It also includes a map of ‘Brampton’ on the side as that this where the company operates from.







1 e-commerce microsite: Live payment – Shop and Cart/Checkout/My Account:The shop page allows users to scroll through the store and add products to their cart. The cart page is used for viewers who would like to see what is in their carts. The checkout page is a dropdown, viewed under the ‘cart’ page. The “my account” is also a dropdown page under cart, so users are able to login and see their accounts on this website.



Accessibility and AODA compliance:



The site ensured to take in consideration accessibility and usability requirements. Using WebAIM (Web accessibility in mind) contrast checker, I was able to make sure that my website colour palette did not impact the readability.
