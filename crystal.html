Designing & Creating An E-Commerce Webpage For
A Crystal Shop


(Crystal Collection)


Roles: UI designer and Web Designer/Developer

Tools: Adobe XD, Dreamweaver, Wordpress

Skills: Wireframing, Prototyping

Time: Two Weeks





Problem:


This business needs a place to sell their products and a space where customers can request items, and contact them easily.

Solution (Goal):


The solution is to create a website that can display their products, location and other customer needs.




About 'Crystal Collection':

This site is for the business Crystal Collection, they sell healing crystals/stones to people who are interested in its healing properties. And to those who find the crystals and stones “aesthetically pleasing” or just for pure decoration.


Plan:

Below are the site's requirements (I was able to successfully implement all.)


Homepage: The homepage should have a working navigation across all the inner pages and feature a mash-up of highlight content from all the inner pages as well as the blog. The featured highlights should be visually appealing and include a carousel slider, connotative background video, and custom map.

1 landing blog page aggregating and linking to a few individual blog posts: There should be a blog which essentially is a page where the business posts reviews they have received (real-time) from their customers via order notes, email or comments left under their videos or images from social media.

1 event registration section – About: The About page should have details about the company, and where they are based from. It also should include information such as pickup, and shipping options. The page also should mention the refund policy for Crystal Collection. In addtion, it should let users to register and become part of the “Crystal Collection family” and to let the company know what crystals the user would like to see in the store. The about page should also have a dropdown menu which leads to the blog.

Packaging Orders: This page will display the company’s TikTok-social media account. The page will embed videos from the company’s social media account which exhibits how they package their crystals. It shows a “unique” side of the company and customers like seeing interactive content.

1 contact information page with integrated form – Contact: The contact page should have the option for users who visit the website to input their personal information to send an email to the company. It also should include a map of ‘Brampton’ on the side as that this where the company operates from.

1 e-commerce microsite: Live payment – Shop and Cart/Checkout/My Account:The shop page should allow users to scroll through the store and add products to their cart. The cart page is used for viewers who would like to see what is in their carts. The checkout page is a dropdown, viewed under the ‘cart’ page. The “my account” is also a dropdown page under cart, so users are able to login and see their accounts on this website.



Information Architecture (Sitemap):



Home Page:

Provides the user with information about company policy.

In the footer, the users will find the social media handles of the shop.

Visual Identity:


The brand’s visual identity is a purple/lilac colour. This is because one of the meanings of this colour, intended to stick with this company and it visual identity with is “happy” and “passion”. Not only does this colour represent this meaning but this colour is very similar to the crystal amethyst. The brand wants the crystal amethyst to remind the customers of the brand as helpful, as the (healing property) amethyst helps with almost everything such as dispelling anger, rage, fear and anxiety. The brand wants customers to feel comfortable when searching the website and to remind them that the company is here for them and is ready to help them with their needs.
